Champurrado

Ingredients

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 tablet (about 3.3 ounces) Mexican chocolate (such as Abuelita or Ibarra), chopped
  • 1/2 cup masa harina (corn dough)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground anise (optional)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Whipped cream (optional, for serving)

Instructions

Step 1: Mix Masa Harina

In a separate bowl, whisk the masa harina with 1 cup of water until you have a smooth, lump-free mixture.

Step 2: Heat Chocolate

In a large saucepan, combine the chopped Mexican chocolate with the remaining 3 cups of water. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring frequently until the chocolate has completely dissolved and the mixture is smooth.

Step 3: Add Spices and Masa Harina

Stir in the ground cinnamon, ground anise (if using), and a pinch of salt. Gradually add the masa harina mixture to the chocolate while stirring constantly.

Step 4: Simmer

Continue to cook the champurrado over medium-low heat, stirring constantly to avoid lumps, until it thickens and reaches your desired consistency. This may take about 15-20 minutes.

Step 5: Add Sugar

Stir in the granulated sugar and adjust the sweetness to your liking. You can add more or less sugar based on your preference.

Step 6: Add Milk

Pour in the milk and continue to cook the champurrado, stirring until itâ€™s heated through. Be careful not to boil it, as it can scorch or curdle the milk.

Step 7: Serve

Ladle the champurrado into cups or mugs. You can garnish it with whipped cream or a sprinkle of ground cinnamon if desired.

Step 8: Enjoy

Serve the champurrado hot and enjoy this rich and comforting Mexican hot chocolate drink!
